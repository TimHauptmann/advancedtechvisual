Gulfstream Aerospace

Gulfstream Aerospace is a leading company in the business jet industry, with a history of innovation and excellence. Gulfstream Aerospace was founded in 1958 as a subsidiary of Grumman Aircraft Engineering Co., which was known for military aircraft production.

The company’s first product was the Gulfstream I (G-I), a twin turboprop business aircraft that could seat 12 passengers and had a range of 2,200 miles. The company later developed the Gulfstream II (GII), the first jet-powered business aircraft, and the Gulfstream III (GIII), which achieved greater range and speed than the GII and was the first business jet to fly over both poles. In 1978, the company was sold to American Jet Industries, headed by entrepreneur Allen Paulson, who renamed it Gulfstream American and made the development of the Gulfstream IV (GIV) a priority. In 1985, the company became independent and changed its name to Gulfstream Aerospace. In 1999, the company was acquired by General Dynamics, a global aerospace and defense company.

The company’s current product line consists of the G280, G550, G500/G600, and G650/G650ER/G700/G800, which are among the most advanced and luxurious business jets in the world. The company also provides a range of services and support for its customers, such as aircraft refurbishment, maintenance, field and airborne support, product enhancements, and special missions. The company has more than 19,000 employees and operates in several locations around the world, including Savannah, Georgia (headquarters), Appleton, Wisconsin, Brunswick, Georgia, Dallas, Texas, Las Vegas, Nevada, London, England, Long Beach, California, West Palm Beach, Florida, and Zurich, Switzerland.
