Turbojet Engine

November 1, 2023

Turbojet engines are air-breathing engines that generate thrust by the continuous expulsion of a high-speed jet of gas from the engine's exhaust nozzle. They work by drawing in air using a compressor, which compresses the air and then mixes it with fuel. The mixture is then ignited, causing it to rapidly expand and shoot out of the exhaust nozzle at high speeds. This generates a force in the opposite direction, which propels the aircraft forward.

The compressor is powered by a turbine, which is turned by the expanding gases created by the combustion process. This creates a self-sustaining cycle of air intake, compression, combustion, and exhaust.

Turbojet engines are most commonly used in high-speed aircraft, such as military fighter jets and commercial airliners. While they are not as fuel-efficient as newer engine designs, they are still widely used due to their ability to generate high levels of thrust and operate at high altitudes and speeds.
