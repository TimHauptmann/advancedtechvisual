Embraer S.A.

Embraer S.A. is a Brazilian company that produces and sells commercial, executive, defense, and security aircraft and related services. Embraer S.A. was founded in 1969 by Ozires Silva, a Brazilian engineer and former air force officer, as a state-owned enterprise to develop and manufacture aircraft for the domestic and regional markets.

The company’s corporate headquarters is located in Sao Jose dos Campos, Sao Paulo, Brazil, but it also has operations in more than 65 countries. The company is organized into four primary divisions: Embraer Commercial Aviation, which manufactures jetliners and corporate jets; Embraer Executive Jets, which produces business jets and private jets; Embraer Defense & Security, which designs and builds military and security aircraft and systems; and Embraer Services & Support, which provides after-sales and maintenance services. The company employs more than 18,000 people across the world and has a global network of service centers.

The company’s most popular commercial and executive aircraft include the E-Jet family of regional jets, the Phenom 100 and 300 families of light jets, the Legacy 450 and 500 families of midsize jets, the Praetor 500 and 600 families of super-midsize jets, and the Lineage 1000 family of ultra-large jets. The company’s most notable defense and security products include the Super Tucano light attack aircraft, the KC-390 multi-mission transport aircraft, the EMB 145 AEW&C airborne early warning and control aircraft, and the Gripen NG fighter jet.
