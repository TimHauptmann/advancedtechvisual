Airbus SE

Airbus SE is a European multinational aerospace corporation that produces and sells commercial aircraft, helicopters, defense and space products, and related services. Airbus SE was founded in 1970 as Airbus Industrie, a consortium of European aerospace companies, to compete with American aircraft manufacturers. The company’s corporate headquarters is located in Leiden, the Netherlands, but its main operational base is in Toulouse, France.

The company is organized into three segments: Airbus, which manufactures commercial aircraft and corporate jets; Airbus Helicopters, which produces civil and military helicopters; and Airbus Defence and Space, which designs and builds satellites, launchers, and defense systems. The company employs more than 140,000 people across the world and has operations in Europe, the Americas, Asia-Pacific, the Middle East, and Africa.

The company’s most popular commercial aircraft include the A320, A330, A350, and A380 families of jetliners and the ACJ range of business jets. The company’s most notable defense and space products include the A400M military transport aircraft, the Eurofighter Typhoon fighter jet, the NH90 helicopter, the Ariane 5 and 6 launchers, and the Galileo navigation system.
