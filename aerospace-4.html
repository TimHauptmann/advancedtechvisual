Turbofan Engine

November 1, 2023

Turbofan engines are air-breathing engines that are a type of gas turbine engine. They work by drawing in air using a fan, which is located at the front of the engine. The fan compresses the air and then splits it into two streams. One stream passes through the engine core, where it is ignited and expelled from the exhaust nozzle at high speeds, generating thrust.

The second stream bypasses the engine core and is instead directed around the outside of the engine. This bypass air provides additional thrust and also helps to cool the engine, making it more efficient.

The fan is powered by a turbine, which is turned by the expanding gases created by the combustion process. This creates a self-sustaining cycle of air intake, compression, combustion, and exhaust.

Turbofan engines are most commonly used in commercial airliners, as they are more fuel-efficient than turbojet engines and can operate at lower altitudes and speeds. They are also quieter than other engine designs, making them more desirable for use in urban areas.
