Commercial Aircraft Corporation of China, Ltd.

Commercial Aircraft Corporation of China, Ltd. (COMAC) is a Chinese state-owned aerospace manufacturer that produces and sells commercial aircraft and related products and services. COMAC was established on May 11, 2008 in Shanghai, China, as the main vehicle for implementing large passenger aircraft programs in China. The company’s corporate headquarters is located in Pudong, Shanghai, but it also has research and manufacturing centers in Beijing, Xi’an, Chengdu, Nanchang, and other cities.

The company is organized into four primary divisions: COMAC Commercial Aircraft, which manufactures jetliners and business jets; COMAC Helicopters, which produces civil and military helicopters; COMAC Defense and Space, which designs and builds satellites, launchers, and defense systems; and COMAC Capital, which provides financial and leasing services. The company employs more than 23,000 people across the world and has operations in Europe, the Americas, Asia-Pacific, the Middle East, and Africa.

The company’s most popular commercial aircraft include the ARJ21, a twin-engine, single-aisle, regional jet that can seat 70 to 105 passengers; and the C919, a twin-engine, single-aisle, short- to medium-range jet that can seat 150 to 190 passengers. The company’s most notable defense and space products include the KJ-2000, an airborne early warning and control aircraft; the Y-20, a large military transport aircraft; the LM-5, a heavy-lift launch vehicle; and the BeiDou, a global navigation satellite system.
