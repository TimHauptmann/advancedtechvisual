Samsung Electronics

Samsung is a South Korean multinational conglomerate that has its headquarters in Seoul. The company was founded by Lee Byung-chul in 1938 as a trading company, and over the years, it has diversified into various fields such as food processing, textiles, insurance, securities, and retail. However, Samsung's electronics division is the most notable, as it produces a vast range of consumer electronics, including smartphones, tablets, laptops, televisions, home appliances, and computer peripherals.

Samsung is one of the largest companies globally by revenue, with its operations spanning across different continents. The company is also a significant player in the semiconductor industry, manufacturing a wide range of chips used in various electronic devices. Samsung has a broad global reach, with operations in more than 80 countries and over 300,000 employees worldwide.

Samsung's commitment to providing innovative and technologically advanced products has made it a household name worldwide. The company invests heavily in research and development to ensure that its products remain competitive in the market. Samsung's vision is to inspire the world with its innovative technologies, products, and designs while contributing to creating a better global society.
