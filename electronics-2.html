Internal Combustion Engine

November 1, 2023

The internal combustion engine is a type of engine that uses the combustion of fuel and air to produce energy. It works by igniting a mixture of fuel and air inside a combustion chamber, which causes a controlled explosion. The explosion creates pressure, which is harnessed by the engine to perform work.

The basic components of an internal combustion engine include a fuel system, an air intake system, a combustion chamber, a piston, and a crankshaft. The fuel system supplies fuel to the engine, while the air intake system supplies air. The fuel and air mix together in the combustion chamber, where they are ignited by a spark plug. This creates a high-pressure gas that pushes the piston down, which in turn rotates the crankshaft. The rotation of the crankshaft is what ultimately powers the vehicle or machine.

There are different types of internal combustion engines, such as gasoline and diesel engines. Gasoline engines use spark plugs to ignite the fuel, while diesel engines use compression to ignite the fuel. Despite the differences, the basic principles of operation remain the same.
